# Target: Dedicated Hub screen(s) for standards and conventions

## Current statement

The Hub should have its own screen(s) for **coding standards and conventions**, presenting
them in a dedicated view rather than leaving them invisible in the repo. The source is the
user-authored standards from `.sdd/standards/` and the `CLAUDE.md` standards section
(SPEC-wf-028).

- Standards/conventions are surfaced in their own screen with a navigation entry, consistent
  with the other Hub screens.
- The view presents the standards content (formatting/style, best-practices/anti-patterns,
  project conventions, architectural rules).

Open decisions to settle at target-engage (not pinned here):
- **One screen vs. separate "standards" and "conventions" screens** (the user said
  "screens" — resolve singular vs. split).
- **Rendered document vs. an item list** — standards are not ID'd spec/gap artifacts
  (per SPEC-wf-028 they live in `.sdd/standards/` + `CLAUDE.md`), so the view may render the
  standards document(s) rather than an artifact list with IDs; decide the presentation.
- **Read-only vs. editable** in the UI, and how that reconciles with the files being the
  source of truth.
- **Data layer**: how the Hub loads `.sdd/standards/` (and/or the `CLAUDE.md` standards
  section).

Scope note: Hub UI (`ui-screens`), depends on the standards artifact existing
(SPEC-wf-028). Exact decomposition settled during target-engage.
