# Target: Complete, authoritative knowledge of how to work with an artifact, at the point of use

## Current statement

Whenever the agent — or the main Claude session — engages or works with an SDD artifact,
it must have complete and authoritative knowledge of how to work with that artifact type:
no gaps, no guessing, no partial application of the rules.

"Complete knowledge of how to work with an artifact" means knowing, for that artifact type:
- its **shape** (schema / required fields, ID convention),
- its **lifecycle** and the valid state transitions,
- the **procedure** for operating on it (the governing skill/rules),
- the **discipline and invariants** it must uphold (e.g. append-only dialog, atomic writes,
  version recomputation, archiving on terminal state),
- and the relevant **edge cases**.

This knowledge must be:
- **complete** — 100% of what is needed to work with the artifact correctly,
- **authoritative and current** — the actual rules, not stale memory or a drifting copy,
- **available at the point of use** — present when a specific artifact is being engaged or
  worked on, for both the autonomous agent and the main session.

Relationship: complements TGT-102 (session-start high-level priming). 102 orients the agent
broadly at the start of a session; this guarantees deep, exhaustive operating knowledge at
the moment a specific artifact is actually touched.

(The *how* — delivery mechanism, single-source-of-truth strategy, and how we verify the
knowledge is actually complete — is to be settled together during engage, not deferred.)

  invariants/discipline, edge cases); `references/schemas.md` becomes an index; a CI/grep
  check enforces the six sections.
