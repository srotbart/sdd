# Target: sdd-worker autonomously drives the full execution loop

## Current statement

When the `sdd-worker` is handed an execution scope, it should drive the full
audit → decompose → close loop to completion autonomously, rather than stopping after each
individual skill. Today every execution skill ends with an interactive `Next: run /sdd:...`
footer aimed at a human stepping through one skill at a time, and there is no single skill
that internally loops; a background worker therefore treats each skill's completion as
end-of-turn and goes idle at the `Next:` boundary instead of chaining to the next step
(observed repeatedly — the worker ran one `gap-to-work-items` and stopped without
`work-item-close`). The intended behavior: spawn the worker, and it carries a domain's work
from spec-audit through every work-item-close without per-step nudging, reporting only at
genuine completion or a real blocker.

Open decisions to settle at target-engage (not pinned here):
- **Mechanism** — force the full loop in the `spawn-sdd-worker` prompt (explicit "do not stop
  at Next: footers; continue until all work items are closed"), vs. a dedicated looping skill
  (e.g. `/sdd:close-domain {domain}`) that internally calls audit → decompose → close, vs. a
  harness-level change. Weigh reliability of each against a background teammate's turn model.
- **Footer semantics** — should pipeline-skill `Next:` footers be advisory-only when invoked
  by the worker, so they don't read as stop points?
- **Stop conditions** — how the worker distinguishes "nothing to do" / a genuine blocker (stop
  and report) from a normal between-skills boundary (continue).
- **Relationship to the manual-drive workaround** — the lead currently drives the worker
  step-by-step and waits for its idle signal (proven to work in practice); the target is to
  remove that need.
